Vocalist / Songwriter / Producer

Lalah Hathaway

Chicago, IL 5x Grammy Winner R&B Royalty

Lalah Hathaway is a five-time Grammy winner and one of the most commanding vocalists in contemporary music. She holds the distinction of winning Best Traditional R&B Performance three times — a record she shares only with Beyoncé. The Chicago-born daughter of the late, legendary Donny Hathaway inherited a once-in-a-generation instrument and built a legacy that stands entirely on its own terms.

Across seven solo albums, Hathaway has moved fluidly between traditional soul, progressive R&B, jazz, and gospel, refusing to let any single category contain her artistry. Her collaborators reflect the scope of her influence — Pharrell Williams, Kendrick Lamar, Dr. Dre, Anderson .Paak, Robert Glasper, Rapsody, Snoop Dogg, and Esperanza Spalding among them. She has been invited to share the stage by Prince, Stevie Wonder, and Herbie Hancock, each of whom recognized in her a peer rather than a protégée.

Her accolades extend well beyond the recording studio. Hathaway earned a spot on the 2021 Oscars shortlist for Best Original Song, claimed the number-one Adult R&B single of 2016, and has delivered landmark performances on Late Show with Stephen Colbert. Billboard, VIBE, Essence, NPR, and The New York Times have each profiled her as a defining voice of modern soul.

What sets Hathaway apart is not merely technical ability — it is the emotional gravity she brings to every note, the sense that each performance carries the full weight of a lineage while pushing restlessly forward. She does not simply honor a tradition; she expands it with every breath.
