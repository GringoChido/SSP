Lalah Hathaway

Chicago, IL 5x Grammy Winner R&B Royalty

There are singers, and then there is Lalah Hathaway. The daughter of the late, legendary Donny Hathaway inherited a once-in-a-generation instrument and proceeded to carve a legacy entirely her own — five Grammy Awards deep, with three wins for Best Traditional R&B Performance, a record she shares only with Beyoncé. When she steps to a microphone, rooms go silent and musicians shake their heads in disbelief.

Across seven solo albums and a career spanning more than three decades, Hathaway has moved fluidly between traditional soul, progressive R&B, jazz, and gospel, refusing to let any single category contain her artistry. Her collaborators read like a hall of fame unto themselves — Pharrell Williams, Kendrick Lamar, Dr. Dre, Anderson .Paak, Robert Glasper, Snoop Dogg, Esperanza Spalding, and Rapsody among them. She has been invited to share the stage by Prince, Stevie Wonder, and Herbie Hancock, each of whom recognized in her a peer rather than a protégée.

Her accolades extend beyond the recording studio. Hathaway earned a spot on the 2021 Oscars shortlist for Best Original Song, claimed the number-one Adult R&B single of 2016, and has delivered landmark performances on Late Show with Stephen Colbert. Billboard, VIBE, Essence, NPR, and The New York Times have all profiled her as a defining voice of contemporary soul.

What sets Hathaway apart is not merely technical ability — it is the emotional gravity she brings to every note, the sense that each performance is a conversation between her father’s towering legacy and her own restless artistic ambition. She does not simply carry a tradition; she expands it with every breath.
