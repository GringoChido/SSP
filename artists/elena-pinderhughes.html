Flutist / Vocalist / Composer

Elena Pinderhughes

Bay Area, CA Award-Winning Manhattan School of Music

Elena Pinderhughes makes the flute sound like it has always belonged at the center of contemporary music — not as an ornament or a novelty, but as a voice of genuine authority. The Bay Area-raised, Manhattan School of Music-trained artist has established herself as one of the most versatile and sought-after musicians of her generation, moving fluidly between jazz stages, hip-hop sessions, film scoring suites, and sold-out arenas without ever losing the thread of her own artistic identity.

Her trajectory has been defined by an extraordinary range of collaborators and contexts. She has performed at Carnegie Hall, the White House, and the Kennedy Center with equal ease, and has toured or recorded with Herbie Hancock, Robert Glasper, Esperanza Spalding, Common, Terrace Martin, Chief Adjuah, and Victoria Monet. Her flute and vocal contributions appear on landmark projects including Chief Adjuah's Stretch Music, Common's Black America Again, and Victoria Monet's Jaguar II.

Pinderhughes's reach extends into film as well. She contributed to the Academy Award-nominated score for American Fiction and has worked within Laura Karpman's composing studio on HBO's Duster, bringing her improvisational sensibility to the exacting demands of screen music.

Now preparing ambitious solo releases that weave together her gifts as a flutist, vocalist, and songwriter, Pinderhughes stands at a pivotal moment. The world has heard what she can do in service of other artists' visions; what comes next will be entirely her own.
