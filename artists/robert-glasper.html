Pianist / Producer / Composer

Robert Glasper

Houston, TX 5x Grammy Winner Apple Music Exclusive

Robert Glasper did not simply build a bridge between jazz and hip-hop — he detonated the barriers separating them and constructed an entirely new landscape from the aftermath. The Houston-born pianist and producer holds five Grammy wins across fifteen nominations spanning nine categories, alongside an Emmy and a Peabody Award, establishing him as one of the most decorated and genre-defiant musicians of his generation.

Glasper’s 2012 album Black Radio fundamentally altered the trajectory of R&B, fusing live jazz instrumentation with the rhythmic vocabulary of hip-hop in ways the industry had never heard. That vision extended into the studio with Kendrick Lamar, where Glasper’s keys became a cornerstone of To Pimp a Butterfly, and into collaborative projects like R+R=Now, August Greene with Common and Karriem Riggins, and the supergroup Dinner Party. His film scoring work for HBO further demonstrated a composer whose ear knows no walls.

In 2024, Glasper entered a new chapter with an exclusive Apple Music partnership, releasing three albums in rapid succession — In December, Let Go, and Code Derivation, the latter earning a 2025 Grammy nomination. The output was prolific even by his own standards, signaling an artist still accelerating rather than coasting on legacy.

Today, Glasper anchors a sixth-season residency at Blue Note NYC and curates the Black Radio Experience festival in Napa Valley, now in its third year. Whether commanding a solo piano or orchestrating a room full of collaborators, he remains the gravitational center of a movement that continues to reshape what modern Black music sounds like.
